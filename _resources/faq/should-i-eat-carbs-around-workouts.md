If training is hard, long, high-volume, or repeated within the same day, carbohydrates around training can help. Carbs support muscle {% include glossary.html id="glycogen" text="glycogen" %}, which matters for repeated hard sets, endurance work, team sports, and mixed conditioning.{% include citation.html id="burke-2011-carbohydrates-for-training-and-competition" label="1" %}

For typical lifting sessions under an hour, you may not need special workout carbs if your daily food is already adequate. For longer or harder sessions, a pre-workout meal with digestible carbs and protein can improve training quality. During sessions that run long or include lots of hard conditioning, a sports drink or simple carbohydrate can be useful.{% include citation.html id="jeukendrup-2010-carbohydrate-and-exercise-performance" label="2" %}

Do not force workout sugar because it sounds athletic. Use it when it solves a performance or recovery problem without pushing calories out of range.
