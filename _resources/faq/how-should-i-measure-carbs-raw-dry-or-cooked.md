Use the form that matches the database entry. If the entry says dry rice, weigh dry rice. If it says cooked rice, weigh cooked rice. The same food can change weight dramatically with water, so mixing dry-weight entries with cooked-weight portions is one of the easiest ways to introduce tracking error.

For foods you batch cook, the simplest method is to weigh the raw or dry amount, cook the full batch, then divide the cooked total into servings. If that is too much friction, choose a reliable cooked entry and use it consistently. Consistency matters more than false precision.
