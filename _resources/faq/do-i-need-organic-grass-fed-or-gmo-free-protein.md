No. Those choices can reflect taste, budget, animal-welfare priorities, environmental concerns, or personal values, but they are not required for muscle gain, fat loss, or a high-quality diet.

For body composition, the first-order questions are simpler: Does the food help you hit protein? Does it fit calories? Can you afford and repeat it? Is it prepared safely? Organic or grass-fed labels do not automatically make a food high-protein, low-calorie, or better matched to your goal.
