Yes. Coffee and tea contribute fluid. {% include glossary.html id="caffeine" text="Caffeine" %} can have a mild diuretic effect, especially in people who are not habituated, but the water in the drink usually more than offsets that in ordinary servings.{% include citation.html id="popkin-2010-water-hydration-and-health" label="1" %}

The bigger issue is timing and dose. Coffee late in the day may hurt sleep even if it helps alertness, and poor sleep can make hunger, training, recovery, and adherence harder. If your caffeine use is steady and sleep is good, coffee and tea can be part of your daily {% include glossary.html id="hydration" text="hydration" %}. If sleep is worse, move caffeine earlier before cutting fluids.
