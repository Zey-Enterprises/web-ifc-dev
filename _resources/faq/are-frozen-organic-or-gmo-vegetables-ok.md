Frozen vegetables are fine, and often excellent. They are convenient, reduce waste, and are usually processed close to harvest. Canned vegetables can also work; just account for added sodium or sauces if they matter for your plan.

Organic is optional. A systematic review did not find strong evidence that organic foods are consistently more nutritious than conventional alternatives, though people may still choose organic for environmental, taste, pesticide, or values-based reasons.{% include citation.html id="smith-spangler-2012-organic-foods-safer-healthier" label="1" %}

GMO status is also not a reason to avoid vegetables by default. The National Academies report on genetically engineered crops did not support broad claims that approved GE crops are inherently unsafe to eat.{% include citation.html id="national-academies-2016-genetically-engineered-crops" label="2" %} For nutrition coaching, the bigger win is eating enough produce in a form you can afford and repeat.
