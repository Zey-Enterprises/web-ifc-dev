No. Organic and GMO-free labels do not create a calorie deficit, increase protein automatically, or guarantee better {% include glossary.html id="satiety" text="satiety" %}. They may matter to you for reasons outside body composition, but they are not required for fat loss.

Organic foods are not consistently more nutritious than conventional foods in a way that changes the basic coaching priorities.{% include citation.html id="smith-spangler-2012-organic-foods-safer-healthier" label="1" %} The broad safety evidence on approved genetically engineered crops also does not justify treating GMO status as a primary nutrition filter.{% include citation.html id="national-academies-2016-genetically-engineered-crops" label="2" %}

Spend attention where it changes outcomes: calories, protein, fiber, food variety, food safety, cost, and repeatability.
