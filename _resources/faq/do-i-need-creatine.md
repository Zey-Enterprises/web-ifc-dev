No supplement is required for progress, but {% include glossary.html id="creatine" text="creatine" %} is one of the better-supported options for strength, power, repeated high-intensity work, and lean-mass gain when paired with training.{% include citation.html id="kreider-2017-issn-creatine-position-stand" label="1" %}

A common simple protocol is 3-5 grams of creatine monohydrate per day. Loading is optional; it saturates stores faster but is not necessary. Early scale increases are common because muscle stores more water as creatine stores rise. That is not fat gain, and it is usually most relevant if you are making weight for a sport or emotionally reactive to normal scale movement.

If you have kidney disease, take medications that affect kidney function, are pregnant, or have a medical condition that makes supplementation higher-stakes, ask your clinician before using it.
