"Net carbs" usually means total carbohydrate minus {% include glossary.html id="dietary-fiber" text="fiber" %}, and sometimes minus some or all {% include glossary.html id="sugar-alcohols" text="sugar alcohols" %}. That can be useful for certain food labels, but it is not a universal physiological law. Different fibers and sugar alcohols have different energy values and digestive effects.

For most body-composition tracking, use the nutrition label or the app entry consistently rather than trying to outsmart every ingredient. If the app counts sugar alcohols as carbohydrate, leave them counted unless you have a specific reason and a consistent method.

Watch tolerance. Large amounts of sugar alcohols, especially from protein bars and sugar-free candy, can cause gas, bloating, cramping, or diarrhea. A food that technically fits the macros but ruins digestion is not a good default.
