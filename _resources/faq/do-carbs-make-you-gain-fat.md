Carbohydrates do not bypass {% include glossary.html id="calorie-balance" text="calorie balance" %}. Fat gain requires sustained energy intake above expenditure over time. A higher-carb diet can cause a quick scale increase because carbohydrate is stored as {% include glossary.html id="glycogen" text="glycogen" %} with water, but that is not the same thing as gaining body fat.

Carb sources still matter. Potatoes, fruit, rice, oats, beans, and whole grains behave differently in appetite and food quality than candy, pastries, and sweetened drinks. The useful question is not "Are carbs bad?" It is "Which carb sources, portions, and timing support my training, hunger, and calorie target?"

Low-carb diets can work when they help someone reduce calories. Higher-carb diets can also work when calories, protein, and adherence are managed. The mechanism is the eating pattern, not a special exemption from energy balance.{% include citation.html id="hall-2015-fat-vs-carb-restriction" label="1" %}
