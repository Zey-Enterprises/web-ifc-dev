Protein needs depend on body size, leanness, training, calorie intake, and goal. People lifting weights and trying to improve body composition usually need more protein than the general sedentary minimum. Higher protein intakes are especially useful during fat loss because they support lean-mass retention and {% include glossary.html id="satiety" text="satiety" %}.{% include citation.html id="helms-2014-a-systematic-review-of-dietary-protein-during-caloric-restriction-in-resistance-trained-lean-athletes" label="1" %}

Most coaching plans work better when protein is distributed across several meals instead of saved for one large dinner. Even distribution is not magic, but it creates more repeated opportunities to support muscle protein synthesis and appetite control.{% include citation.html id="mamerow-2014-dietary-protein-distribution-positively-influences-24-h-muscle-protein-synthesis-in-healthy-adults" label="2" %}

If you have kidney disease or another medical condition requiring protein restriction, follow your clinician's guidance rather than generic fitness targets.
