Nighttime hunger often means the day was underfed, under-proteined, too low in fiber, too stressful, or too loosely structured. Fix the day before trying to win the night with discipline. A larger lunch, more protein at dinner, more vegetables or potatoes, or a planned evening snack can make a large difference.

If nighttime hunger is mostly habit or stress, change the cue pattern. Close the kitchen after a planned snack, brush teeth, use a non-food decompression routine, and keep highly cue-driven foods out of the default environment. If hunger is severe every night, the calorie deficit may be too aggressive.
