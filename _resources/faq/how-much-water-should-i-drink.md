There is no universal glass-count that works for every body, climate, diet, and training load. Start with thirst, urine color, and performance. If you are rarely thirsty, urine is pale yellow, training feels normal, and you are not getting headaches or cramps, your fluid intake is probably close enough.

Needs rise with sweat, heat, altitude, high-fiber diets, higher protein intake, salty meals, and illness. They can also fall when food intake includes more soups, fruit, vegetables, yogurt, and other water-rich foods.{% include citation.html id="popkin-2010-water-hydration-and-health" label="1" %}

Do not force extreme water intake. Overdoing water without enough {% include glossary.html id="electrolytes" text="electrolytes" %}, especially during long endurance events, can be dangerous. The target is adequate hydration, not maximal water consumption.
